Emotions & Personality | Social Behavior & Interaction

  • Introvert, Ambivert, Extrovert
    • Root: verto = to turn
    • Prefix: ambi = both
    • Someone who can turn both inwards and outwards (i.e. has both tendencies)

  • retiring
    • aloof, distant, preferring to be alone

    • Focusing attention away from oneself, shy, coy

    • friendly and outgoing

  • Collegial
    • warm, friendly

    • Root: vivo = to live (vita = life)

    • Root: avunculus = uncle

    • teasing

  • arch
    • deliberately teasing, tantalizing
    • Example: She was arch, making playful jokes.
  • Sinister vs Gauche
    • Latin Root: sinister = left hand
    • French Root: Gauche = left hand

    • Variants & Related Words: gaucherie

  • Perspicacious vs. Perspicuous
    • Root: specto = to look
    • Prefix: per = through
    • A perspicacious person is keen minded, smart, mentally sharp. Can see through things. A perspicuous thing is clear and easily understood from one look.
    • Variants & Related Words: spectacle, spectacular, spectator, inspect, retrospect, introspect, prospect, circumspect, conspicuous, specious, discernible
  • Ingenuous vs. ingenious
    • Igenuous means frank, open, innocent, clueless, not apt to hide feelings/thoughts. Though ingenuous is complementary, its synonyms credulous, naive and gullible are not.
    • Ingenious means shrewd and clever.
    • Variants & Related Words: credulous, disingenuous
  • Venal vs. Venial
    • open to bribery and corruption, vs forgivable (as in sin)

    • being satisfied with oneself or not thinking about the future, vs being eager to please
    • Example: after receiving straight As, he grew complacent and started turning his homework late. The normally complaisant Jenny refused to do any more chores
  • Allude vs. Elude
    • to imply, vs. to evade capture
    • Example: for three weeks, the bank thieves eluded the police, until a helicopter spotted their hideout spot.
  • Censor vs. censure
    • to purge objectionable material from something, vs. to castigate, berate and criticize harshly
  • Elicit vs illicit
    • to evoke, trigger, or draw out a reaction or, vs. illegal
    • Example: his puns never fail to elicit a laugh from his friends. Illicit drug trades have intensified in recent years.
  • Prescribe vs. proscribe
    • to recommend something, vs to ban something
  • Vacillate vs Oscillate
    • Root: vacillo = to swing back and forth
    • To vacillate is to swing back and forth in indecision, be irresolute, and not being able to make up one’s mind. Vacillate is to swing emotionally and mentally while oscillate is to swing back and forth physically.

  • Clandestine vs. Surreptitious
    • Root: clam = secretly
    • Secrecy; concealment of a plan that is dangerous or illegal. Surreptitious also means secretive, stealthy, and sneaky because of fear of being detected.
    • Cannot be always used interchangeably. Clandestine meetings/arrangements vs surreptitious movements/actions.
  • Adulate vs. Flattery vs. Compliment
    • To adulate is to worship and idolize someone

  • Predecessor vs. Precursor
    • Predecessor is someone who comes before another person while Precursor is something that comes before another thing.
