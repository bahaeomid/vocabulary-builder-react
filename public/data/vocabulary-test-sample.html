General Vocabulary | Personality & Character

  • ephemeral
    • Lasting for a very short time; transitory
    • Example: The ephemeral beauty of cherry blossoms reminds us to appreciate fleeting moments.
    • Variants & Related Words: transient, fleeting, momentary, evanescent
  • ubiquitous
    • Present, appearing, or found everywhere
    • Example: Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society.
    • Variants & Related Words: omnipresent, pervasive, universal
  • querulous
    • Complaining in a petulant or whining manner
    • Example: The querulous customer demanded to speak with the manager about every minor inconvenience.
    • Variants & Related Words: petulant, peevish, fretful
  • stoic
    • Enduring pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaining
    • Example: She maintained a stoic expression throughout the difficult trial.
    • Variants & Related Words: impassive, unemotional, phlegmatic

General Vocabulary | Emotions & Attitudes

  • effervescent
    • Enthusiastic and vivacious; bubbling with excitement
    • Example: Her effervescent personality made her the life of every party.
    • Variants & Related Words: vivacious, bubbly, exuberant
  • mellifluous
    • Sweet-sounding; pleasant to hear
    • Example: The singer's mellifluous voice captivated the entire audience.
    • Variants & Related Words: dulcet, euphonious, melodious
  • querulous
    • Given to complaining; whining
    • Example: The querulous tone of his voice grated on everyone's nerves.
    • Variants & Related Words: peevish, petulant, captious

Academic Words | Words About Words

  • ambiguous
    • Open to more than one interpretation; having a double meaning
    • Example: The politician's ambiguous statement could be interpreted in many ways.
    • Variants & Related Words: equivocal, cryptic, enigmatic
  • circumlocution
    • The use of many words where fewer would do; indirect or roundabout expression
    • Example: His circumlocution made it difficult to understand his main point.
    • Variants & Related Words: periphrasis, roundabout, indirect
  • laconic
    • Using very few words; concise to the point of seeming rude
    • Example: His laconic reply suggested he was not interested in continuing the conversation.
    • Variants & Related Words: terse, succinct, pithy, concise
